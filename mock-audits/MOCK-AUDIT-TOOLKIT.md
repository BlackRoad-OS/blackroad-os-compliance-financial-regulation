## üéØ AUDIT SCOPE - COMPLETE EXAMINATION

### 1. BOOKS AND RECORDS EXAMINATION

**Sample Size:** Minimum 25 accounts or 10% of total (whichever greater)

#### Account Documentation Review

```
For Each Sample Account, Verify:

‚ñ° New Account Form complete and accurate
  - All required information present
  - Customer signature and date
  - Principal approval signature and date (within 30 days)
  - Approval date reasonable
  
‚ñ° Customer Identification Program (CIP)
  - Copy of government-issued ID on file
  - Address verification documented
  - OFAC screening performed
  - Beneficial ownership form (if applicable)

‚ñ° Suitability Documentation
  - Investment objectives clearly stated
  - Risk tolerance assessed and documented
  - Financial information complete (income, net worth, liquid net worth)
  - Investment experience documented
  - Tax status recorded

‚ñ° Account Agreements
  - Customer agreement signed
  - Margin agreement (if applicable) - signed before first margin trade
  - Options agreement (if applicable) - approved by options principal
  - Discretionary authorization (if applicable) - written and signed

‚ñ° Ongoing Maintenance
  - Account information updated at least every 36 months
  - Material changes documented
  - Trusted contact person designated (if after 2/3/2018)
```

### 2. TRADE SUPERVISION EXAMINATION

**Sample Period:** Most recent 3 months  
**Sample Size:** Minimum 100 trades across all account types

#### Trade Review Matrix

```
For Each Sample Trade:

‚ñ° Order Ticket Complete
  - Date and time of order
  - Account number/identifier
  - Security description
  - Number of shares/units
  - Buy or sell
  - Type of order (market, limit, stop, etc.)
  - Price (if limit or stop)
  - Customer identification/account executive
  - Solicited or unsolicited
  - Discretionary indicator (if applicable)

‚ñ° Suitability Review
  - Trade consistent with customer's investment objectives
  - Trade consistent with customer's risk tolerance
  - Trade consistent with customer's financial situation
  - Concentration risk assessed
  - Principal review documented (if required)

‚ñ° Best Execution
  - Execution price reasonable
  - Execution compared to NBBO (National Best Bid/Offer)
  - Execution quality reviewed
  - Any payment for order flow disclosed

‚ñ° Timely Settlement
  - Settlement date correct (T+2 for equities/corporate bonds)
  - Money/securities delivered on time
  - Failed trades documented and resolved
```

### 3. SUPERVISION SYSTEM REVIEW

#### Written Supervisory Procedures (WSP)

```
WSP Comprehensiveness Check:

‚ñ° Designation of Principals
  - All supervisory principals designated
  - Appropriate series licenses for supervision
  - Succession plan documented

‚ñ° Supervisory Structure
  - Organization chart current
  - Reporting lines clear
  - Span of control reasonable

‚ñ° Review Procedures
  - Email/correspondence review procedures
  - Trade review procedures
  - Account approval procedures
  - Advertising review procedures
  - Outside business activity review
  - Private securities transactions review

‚ñ° Branch Office Supervision
  - OSJ designation procedures
  - Non-OSJ branch procedures
  - Residential office procedures
  - Inspection frequency schedules

‚ñ° Annual Review
  - WSP reviewed and updated annually
  - Changes documented
  - All personnel acknowledged updated WSP
```

#### Supervisory Implementation

```
Evidence of Supervision:

‚ñ° Principal Reviews
  - New account approvals timely (within 30 days)
  - Trade reviews documented
  - Exception reports reviewed and signed
  - Frequency appropriate to risk

‚ñ° Branch Inspections
  - OSJs inspected annually
  - Non-OSJ branches inspected within 3 years
  - Inspection reports on file
  - Deficiencies documented and remediated
  - Follow-up documented

‚ñ° Correspondence Review
  - Sample of emails reviewed
  - Review frequency documented
  - Prohibited content would be detected
  - Social media monitored

‚ñ° Exception Reports
  - Churning reports run monthly
  - Unauthorized trading alerts reviewed
  - Concentrated position reports reviewed
  - Action taken on exceptions documented
```

### 4. ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) PROGRAM

```
AML Program Review:

‚ñ° Written AML Program
  - Policies and procedures comprehensive
  - Risk assessment conducted and documented
  - Updated annually
  - Board approval documented

‚ñ° AML Compliance Officer
  - Designated AML officer identified
  - Appropriate authority and resources
  - Reports to senior management

‚ñ° Independent Testing
  - Testing conducted annually
  - Conducted by independent party
  - Report on file
  - Deficiencies remediated

‚ñ° Training
  - All personnel trained at hire
  - Annual AML training conducted
  - Training content appropriate
  - Attendance documented

‚ñ° Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
  - CIP procedures followed
  - Enhanced due diligence for high-risk customers
  - Beneficial ownership identified
  - PEP screening conducted

‚ñ° Suspicious Activity Reporting
  - Transaction monitoring system in place
  - SARs filed timely (within 30 days of detection)
  - SAR filing documentation on file
  - No customer notification (confidentiality maintained)

‚ñ° Currency Transaction Reporting
  - CTRs filed for cash transactions $10,000+
  - Filed within 15 days
  - Structuring monitored and prevented
```

### 5. ADVERTISING AND COMMUNICATIONS

**Sample Size:** All retail communications from past 3 months

```
Advertising Review:

‚ñ° Principal Approval
  - All retail communications pre-approved by principal
  - Approval date before first use
  - Principal signature/electronic approval

‚ñ° FINRA Filing
  - Required communications filed with FINRA
  - Filed within 10 days of first use (or 10 days before for investment companies)
  - Filing confirmation retained

‚ñ° Content Standards
  - No false or misleading statements
  - No exaggerated claims
  - No omissions of material facts
  - Balanced presentation (risks and benefits)
  - No predictions or projections (unless appropriate)
  - No testimonials (unless compliant)

‚ñ° Specific Product Communications
  - Options communications compliant (ODD delivered)
  - Investment company communications (rankings compliant)
  - Bond communications (yield disclosures appropriate)
  - Mutual fund performance (standardized calculations)

‚ñ° Social Media
  - Social media policy in place
  - Static vs interactive content distinguished
  - Third-party posts monitored
  - Attestation records retained
```

### 6. CUSTOMER COMPLAINTS

**Review Period:** All complaints past 12 months

```
Complaint Handling Review:

‚ñ° Complete Complaint File
  - Written complaint retained
  - Date received documented
  - Associated person identified
  - Activities complained about documented
  - Action taken (if any) documented

‚ñ° Investigation
  - Complaint investigated promptly
  - Investigation documented
  - Customer received response
  - Principal reviewed

‚ñ° Regulatory Reporting
  - U4/U5 updated if $15,000+ claim
  - Updated within 30 days
  - Arbitration/litigation reported
  - Criminal actions reported

‚ñ° Pattern Analysis
  - Multiple complaints against same rep identified
  - Systemic issues identified
  - Corrective action taken
```

### 7. FINANCIAL RESPONSIBILITY

```
Net Capital Review (Broker-Dealers):

‚ñ° Daily Net Capital Calculation
  - Calculated daily
  - Maintained above minimum ($250,000 general, $5,000 introducing)
  - Aggregate indebtedness ratio <15:1
  - Early warning notification if <120% of minimum

‚ñ° Customer Protection Rule (SEC 15c3-3)
  - Reserve computation weekly
  - Segregation of customer securities
  - Good control location
  - Possession or control location

‚ñ° Annual Financial Audit
  - PCAOB-registered auditor
  - Filed timely with SEC/FINRA
  - Unqualified opinion
  - FOCUS reports filed monthly/quarterly
```

```
Investment Adviser Financial Review:

‚ñ° Form ADV Part 1A
  - Assets under management accurate
  - Fee schedule current
  - Disciplinary history complete
  - Filed within 90 days of fiscal year-end

‚ñ° Custody Compliance
  - Qualified custodian used
  - Quarterly statements sent
  - Surprise examination conducted (if required)
  - Balance sheet filed (if required)

‚ñ° Minimum Net Worth
  - $35,000 if custody
  - $10,000 if discretion
  - Or surety bond in lieu
```

## üéØ RISK-BASED TESTING APPROACH

### High-Risk Areas (Enhanced Testing)

1. **Options Trading**
   - 100% of option accounts reviewed
   - ODD delivery verified
   - Suitability heightened
   - Principal approval verified

2. **Senior Investors (65+)**
   - Enhanced suitability review
   - Concentration risk assessment
   - Liquidity needs verification
   - Diminished capacity indicators

3. **Discretionary Accounts**
   - Written authorization verified
   - Trading pattern review
   - Churning analysis
   - Principal oversight documented

4. **Private Placements/Reg D**
   - Accredited investor verification
   - Suitability documentation extensive
   - Private placement memorandum on file
   - Subscription agreements complete

5. **Complex Products**
   - Structured products
   - Non-traded REITs
   - Alternative investments
   - Enhanced disclosure requirements

## üìä AUDIT FINDINGS CLASSIFICATION

### Finding Severity Matrix

| Severity | Definition | Examples | Required Action |
|----------|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| **CRITICAL** | Immediate regulatory risk, customer harm | Missing AML program, Unregistered persons, Fraud | Immediate cease and remediate |
| **HIGH** | Significant compliance gap | Failed inspections, Missing WSP elements, Churning | Remediate within 30 days |
| **MEDIUM** | Compliance weakness | Documentation gaps, Minor supervision lapses | Remediate within 90 days |
| **LOW** | Best practice improvement | Process inefficiencies, Minor documentation issues | Address in annual planning |
