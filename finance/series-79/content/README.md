# Series 79: Investment Banking Representative Examination

## Overview
The Series 79 exam qualifies individuals to engage in investment banking activities, including underwriting securities, participating in public offerings, and advising on mergers and acquisitions. This is a specialized exam for those working in the investment banking division of a broker-dealer.

## Regulatory Authority
- **FINRA** - Financial Industry Regulatory Authority
- **SEC** - Securities and Exchange Commission
- **MSRB** - Municipal Securities Rulemaking Board (for municipal offerings)

## Who Takes Series 79?

### Investment Banking Professionals
**For those who want to:**
- Underwrite securities offerings (IPOs, secondary offerings)
- Participate in mergers and acquisitions
- Advise corporations on capital raising
- Engage in corporate finance activities
- Work in the investment banking division

### Prerequisites:
- **Series 79 + SIE** = Investment banking representative
- Does NOT require Series 7
- Specialized track separate from retail brokerage

## Key Differences from Other Series

### Series 79 vs. Series 7
- **Series 79**: Investment banking (underwriting, M&A, corporate finance)
- **Series 7**: Retail brokerage (selling to individual investors)
- **Overlap**: Both involve securities knowledge, but different activities

### Series 79 vs. Series 24
- **Series 79**: Investment banking representative activities
- **Series 24**: Supervision of representatives (including Series 79 reps)
- **Relationship**: Series 24 holders can supervise Series 79 activities

### Series 79 vs. Series 63/65/66
- **Series 79**: Federal FINRA exam for investment banking
- **Series 63/65/66**: State law exams for agents and advisers
- **Different Focus**: Series 79 is corporate finance, others are client-facing

## Investment Banking Functions

### Issuer-Side Services
- Advisory on capital structure
- IPO preparation and execution
- Follow-on offering management
- Debt issuance (bonds, notes)
- Private placement facilitation
- M&A advisory and execution

### Underwriting Commitments
- **Firm Commitment**: Underwriter buys entire offering and resells
- **Best Efforts**: Underwriter acts as agent, no purchase commitment
- **All or None**: Must sell all securities or deal cancelled
- **Mini-Max**: Minimum sold or cancelled, maximum cap

### Deal Phases
1. **Origination** - Pitch and win the mandate
2. **Due Diligence** - Investigate and verify information
3. **Structuring** - Determine security type and terms
4. **Marketing** - Roadshow and investor presentations
5. **Pricing** - Determine final offering price
6. **Allocation** - Distribute securities to investors
7. **Aftermarket** - Stabilization and support
