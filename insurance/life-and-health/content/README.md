# Life and Health Insurance License Examination

## Overview
The Life and Health Insurance License is a **state-regulated insurance producer license** that qualifies individuals to sell life insurance products, health insurance policies, annuities, and related insurance products. Unlike FINRA securities exams, this is governed by individual state insurance departments and the National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC).

## Regulatory Authority
- **State Insurance Departments** - Individual state regulators
- **NAIC** - National Association of Insurance Commissioners (model laws)
- **State Insurance Commissioners** - Chief regulatory officers in each state

## Who Takes Life and Health Insurance Licensing?

### Insurance Agents and Producers
**For those who want to:**
- Sell life insurance policies
- Sell health and medical insurance
- Sell disability income insurance
- Sell long-term care insurance
- Sell annuity products
- Work as independent agents or with insurance companies

### License Types:
- **Life Insurance License** - Sell life insurance and annuities only
- **Health Insurance License** - Sell health, disability, LTC only
- **Life AND Health License** - Combined license for all products (most common)
- **Property & Casualty** - Different exam (auto, home, commercial)

## Content Covered

### 1. Life Insurance Products
- **Term Life Insurance** - Temporary coverage (level term, decreasing term)
- **Whole Life Insurance** - Permanent coverage with cash value
- **Universal Life** - Flexible premium permanent insurance
- **Variable Life** - Investment-based permanent insurance
- **Variable Universal Life** - Flexible + investment features
- **Endowment Policies** - Maturity value coverage

### 2. Health Insurance Coverage
- **Major Medical** - Comprehensive health coverage
- **Hospital Indemnity** - Fixed benefits for hospitalization
- **Surgical Expense** - Coverage for surgical procedures
- **PPO (Preferred Provider Organization)** - Network-based care
- **HMO (Health Maintenance Organization)** - Managed care
- **POS (Point of Service)** - Hybrid PPO/HMO plans

### 3. Disability Insurance
- **Short-Term Disability** - Coverage for temporary disabilities
- **Long-Term Disability** - Extended disability protection
- **Elimination Period** - Waiting period before benefits begin
- **Benefit Period** - Duration of disability payments
- **Own Occupation vs. Any Occupation** - Definition of disability
- **Residual Disability** - Partial disability benefits

### 4. Long-Term Care Insurance
- **Nursing Home Care** - Skilled nursing facility coverage
- **Assisted Living** - Residential care facility benefits
- **Home Health Care** - In-home nursing and assistance
- **Adult Day Care** - Daytime supervision services
- **Benefit Triggers** - Activities of Daily Living (ADLs)
- **Inflation Protection** - Cost-of-living adjustments

### 5. Annuities
- **Fixed Annuities** - Guaranteed interest rate
- **Variable Annuities** - Investment-based returns (securities license required)
- **Immediate Annuities** - Income starts immediately
- **Deferred Annuities** - Accumulation phase before income
- **Indexed Annuities** - Returns linked to market index
- **Payout Options** - Life, period certain, joint life

### 6. Policy Provisions
- **Incontestable Clause** - 2-year contestability period
- **Grace Period** - Time to pay overdue premiums (typically 30-31 days)
- **Reinstatement** - Restoring lapsed policies
- **Nonforfeiture Options** - Cash value, extended term, reduced paid-up
- **Policy Loans** - Borrowing against cash value
- **Automatic Premium Loan** - Prevents lapse using cash value

### 7. Beneficiaries
- **Primary Beneficiary** - First in line to receive death benefit
- **Contingent Beneficiary** - Secondary beneficiary if primary predeceases
- **Revocable Beneficiary** - Can be changed by policyowner
- **Irrevocable Beneficiary** - Cannot be changed without consent
- **Per Stirpes** - Distribution to descendants
- **Per Capita** - Equal distribution among survivors

### 8. Underwriting and Risk Assessment
- **Medical Underwriting** - Health history evaluation
- **Paramedical Exam** - Physical examination
- **Attending Physician's Statement (APS)** - Medical records request
- **Risk Classification** - Preferred, standard, substandard, declined
- **Adverse Selection** - High-risk individuals seeking coverage
- **Suicide Clause** - Typically 2-year exclusion

### 9. Premiums and Reserves
- **Level Premium** - Same payment throughout policy
- **Flexible Premium** - Variable payment amounts (universal life)
- **Single Premium** - One-time lump sum payment
- **Cash Value** - Savings component of permanent insurance
- **Surrender Value** - Amount received if policy cancelled
- **Legal Reserve** - Funds insurers must maintain

### 10. Riders and Endorsements
- **Waiver of Premium** - Premiums waived if disabled
- **Accidental Death Benefit (ADB)** - Double indemnity rider
- **Guaranteed Insurability** - Purchase additional coverage without evidence
- **Accelerated Death Benefit** - Early payout for terminal illness
- **Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA)** - Inflation protection
- **Return of Premium** - Refund if no claims filed

### 11. Medicare and Social Security
- **Medicare Part A** - Hospital insurance
- **Medicare Part B** - Medical insurance
- **Medicare Part C** - Medicare Advantage plans
- **Medicare Part D** - Prescription drug coverage
- **Medigap/Medicare Supplement** - Private insurance to fill gaps
- **Social Security Disability Income (SSDI)** - Government disability benefits

### 12. State Insurance Regulations
- **State Insurance Code** - State-specific insurance laws
- **Insurance Commissioner Authority** - Regulatory powers
- **Unfair Trade Practices** - Prohibited activities
- **Privacy Protection** - HIPAA compliance
- **Replacement Regulations** - Rules for replacing existing policies
- **Free Look Period** - Right to cancel (typically 10-30 days)

### 13. Licensing Requirements
- **Prelicensing Education** - Required hours (varies by state, typically 20-40 hours)
- **State Exam** - Pass state insurance licensing exam
- **Fingerprinting** - Background check requirement
- **Continuing Education (CE)** - Ongoing education (varies by state, typically 20-24 hours every 2 years)
- **License Renewal** - Periodic renewal (typically every 2 years)
- **Appointment** - Authorization to represent specific insurers

### 14. Ethics and Practices
- **Fiduciary Duty** - Acting in client's best interest
- **Disclosure Requirements** - Full and honest disclosure
- **Replacement** - Proper procedures for policy replacement
- **Rebating** - Prohibited (giving premium kickbacks)
- **Twisting** - Prohibited (misrepresenting policies to induce replacement)
- **Churning** - Prohibited (excessive replacement for commissions)
- **Controlled Business** - Limits on selling to self/family

### 15. Group Insurance
- **Group Life Insurance** - Employee benefit coverage
- **Group Health Insurance** - Employer-sponsored health plans
- **COBRA** - Continuation of coverage after employment ends
- **HIPAA** - Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act
- **Certificate of Insurance** - Individual's proof of group coverage
- **Conversion Privilege** - Converting group to individual coverage

### 16. Taxation of Insurance
- **Death Benefit** - Generally income tax-free
- **Cash Value Growth** - Tax-deferred accumulation
- **Policy Loans** - Generally not taxable
- **Surrenders** - Gain above basis is taxable
- **Modified Endowment Contract (MEC)** - Tax-disadvantaged status
- **1035 Exchange** - Tax-free policy exchange
- **Annuity Taxation** - Ordinary income on gains

### 17. Claims and Settlement
- **Proof of Loss** - Documentation required for claim
- **Claims Process** - Steps to file and process claims
- **Settlement Options** - Methods of receiving death benefit
- **Lump Sum** - One-time payment
- **Installments** - Fixed period or fixed amount payments
- **Interest Only** - Proceeds left on deposit, interest paid
- **Life Income** - Payments for lifetime

### 18. Employer Retirement Plans
- **401(k) Plans** - Employee salary deferral plans
- **403(b) Plans** - Tax-sheltered annuities for nonprofits
- **Pension Plans** - Defined benefit plans
- **Profit Sharing** - Employer contribution plans
- **ERISA** - Employee Retirement Income Security Act
- **Qualified Plans** - Tax-advantaged retirement plans
- **Vesting** - Ownership of employer contributions

## Key Differences from Securities Licensing

### Insurance vs. Securities
- **Insurance License**: State-regulated, sell insurance products
- **Securities License (FINRA)**: Federally-regulated, sell investment securities
- **Variable Products**: Require BOTH insurance AND securities licenses (Series 6 or 7)

### Regulatory Structure
- **Insurance**: 50 different state regulators + NAIC model laws
- **Securities**: Federal (SEC/FINRA) + state (NASAA/state securities)
- **Reciprocity**: Some states accept licenses from other states

### Products Overlap
- **Variable Annuities**: Insurance license + Series 6/7 required
- **Variable Life Insurance**: Insurance license + Series 6/7 required
- **Fixed Annuities**: Insurance license only
- **Fixed Life Insurance**: Insurance license only

## State-Specific Requirements

Each state has its own:
- Prelicensing education hours
- Exam content (state-specific portion)
- Continuing education requirements
- License renewal periods
- Appointment procedures
- Resident vs. non-resident rules

**Example variations:**
- **California**: 52 hours prelicensing, 24 CE hours every 2 years
- **Texas**: 20 hours prelicensing, 24 CE hours every 2 years
- **Florida**: 40 hours prelicensing, 24 CE hours every 2 years
- **New York**: 75 hours prelicensing (life), 75 hours (health), 15 CE hours annually
