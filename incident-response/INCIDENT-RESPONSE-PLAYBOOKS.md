### PLAYBOOK 1: SEC/FINRA EXAMINATION NOTICE

**IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (Within 1 Hour):**

```
HOUR 1: EMERGENCY RESPONSE

â–¡ Notify immediately:
  - Chief Compliance Officer
  - Chief Executive Officer
  - Legal Counsel (internal or external)
  - All principals

â–¡ Preserve all documents
  - STOP all document destruction
  - Suspend routine document retention
  - Preserve all electronic communications
  - Back up all systems

â–¡ Create examination team:
  - Examination coordinator (usually CCO)
  - Document custodian
  - Legal liaison
  - IT coordinator

â–¡ Secure examination space:
  - Dedicated conference room
  - Secure document storage
  - Separate from normal operations
  - IT access setup

â–¡ Emergency meeting:
  - All principals convened
  - Review examination notice
  - Identify scope and requested materials
  - Assign responsibilities
```

**FIRST 24 HOURS:**

```
DAY 1: INITIAL PREPARATION

â–¡ Analyze examination request
  - Document all requested items
  - Create production checklist
  - Identify potential issues
  - Prioritize high-risk areas

â–¡ Assemble response team:
  - CCO (lead)
  - Legal counsel
  - Operations manager
  - IT manager
  - External consultant (if needed)

â–¡ Document collection:
  - Begin gathering requested documents
  - Organize by category
  - Create document index
  - No alterations or modifications

â–¡ Personnel briefing:
  - All staff notified (no alarm)
  - Interview protocols established
  - Document preservation emphasized
  - Questions routed to CCO only

â–¡ Legal strategy session:
  - Identify potential exposure
  - Prepare privilege log
  - Determine document production strategy
  - Brief all attorneys

â–¡ Communication plan:
  - Internal: Information control
  - External: Media/customer inquiries
  - Regulatory: Point person designated
```

**FIRST WEEK:**

```
WEEK 1: EXAMINATION PREPARATION

â–¡ Document production:
  - Organize all requested materials
  - Create comprehensive index
  - Label clearly and consistently
  - Privilege review completed

â–¡ Practice interviews:
  - Mock interviews for key personnel
  - Testimony coaching
  - Key message points
  - Don't volunteer information

â–¡ Issue identification:
  - Conduct internal investigation
  - Identify weaknesses proactively
  - Prepare explanations
  - Develop remediation plans

â–¡ Daily status meetings:
  - Track document production
  - Monitor examination progress
  - Adjust strategy as needed
  - Communication with legal counsel

â–¡ Regulatory cooperation:
  - Respond to all requests timely
  - Professional and courteous
  - Document all interactions
  - Request extensions if needed (with explanation)
```

### PLAYBOOK 2: CUSTOMER FRAUD ALLEGATION

**IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (Within 30 Minutes):**

```
EMERGENCY FRAUD RESPONSE

â–¡ IMMEDIATE FREEZE:
  - Stop all trading in suspect accounts
  - Preserve all records (no deletions)
  - Freeze employee access if employee suspected
  - Notify bank to freeze wire transfers

â–¡ IMMEDIATE NOTIFICATIONS:
  - CCO (emergency contact)
  - Legal counsel
  - CEO
  - Fidelity bond insurer (if customer funds involved)

â–¡ PRESERVE EVIDENCE:
  - All emails (employee + customer)
  - All trade tickets
  - All account documents
  - All recordings (phone/video)
  - Computer forensic image (if needed)

â–¡ ISOLATE PERSONNEL:
  - Suspect employee suspended immediately
  - Remove system access
  - Escort from premises
  - No contact with customers/witnesses
  - No document access

â–¡ CUSTOMER CONTACT:
  - Acknowledge complaint received
  - DO NOT admit liability
  - Explain investigation process
  - Provide timeline for response
  - Document all communication
```

**FIRST 24 HOURS:**

```
FRAUD INVESTIGATION PROTOCOL

â–¡ Assemble investigation team:
  - External investigator (recommended)
  - Legal counsel
  - Forensic accountant (if funds involved)
  - CCO

â–¡ Evidence collection:
  - Complete account history
  - All communications
  - Wire transfer records
  - Check images
  - Trading records

â–¡ Witness identification:
  - Customer(s)
  - Employees with knowledge
  - Third parties
  - Prioritize interviews

â–¡ Preliminary assessment:
  - Estimated customer loss
  - Potential firm liability
  - Regulatory reporting obligations
  - Insurance coverage analysis

â–¡ Regulatory notifications:
  - FINRA (if member firm)
  - SEC (if investment adviser)
  - State regulators
  - Law enforcement (if criminal)
  - Within 24 hours of discovery
```

**FIRST WEEK:**

```
FRAUD REMEDIATION

â–¡ Complete investigation:
  - Interview all witnesses
  - Analyze all evidence
  - Determine facts
  - Document findings

â–¡ Customer remediation:
  - Calculate actual damages
  - Determine firm responsibility
  - Make restitution offer
  - Document settlement

â–¡ Employee action:
  - Termination (if appropriate)
  - Form U5 filing
  - Criminal referral (if appropriate)
  - Civil recovery action

â–¡ Regulatory filings:
  - FINRA Form U5 (within 30 days)
  - SAR filing (if appropriate)
  - Regulatory investigation cooperation
  - Annual compliance report documentation

â–¡ System improvements:
  - Identify control failures
  - Implement enhanced controls
  - Update supervisory procedures
  - Additional training
```

### PLAYBOOK 3: INSIDER TRADING ALLEGATION

**IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (Within 15 Minutes):**

```
INSIDER TRADING EMERGENCY RESPONSE

â–¡ TRADING HALT:
  - Stop all trading in affected security
  - Stop all trading in related accounts
  - Preserve all trade records
  - NO CANCELLATIONS OR MODIFICATIONS

â–¡ IMMEDIATE ESCALATION:
  - Legal Counsel (FIRST CALL - Attorney-Client Privilege)
  - CCO
  - CEO
  - DO NOT NOTIFY SUSPECT

â–¡ EVIDENCE PRESERVATION:
  - All emails (employee + external)
  - All phone records
  - All trading records
  - All research/analysis
  - Visitor logs
  - Calendar/meetings

â–¡ LEGAL HOLD:
  - Issue litigation hold notice
  - IT: Freeze all suspect communications
  - HR: Preserve personnel files
  - Operations: Freeze all trading records

â–¡ ISOLATE COMMUNICATIONS:
  - All discussions via legal counsel
  - Attorney-client privilege
  - No written communications
  - No admissions
```

**FIRST 24 HOURS (All Through Legal Counsel):**

```
LEGAL-LED INVESTIGATION

â–¡ External counsel engagement:
  - White-collar defense attorney
  - Securities litigation specialist
  - Immediate engagement

â–¡ Parallel SEC investigation:
  - Assume SEC already investigating
  - Prepare for document requests
  - Prepare for testimony
  - Voluntary disclosure decision

â–¡ Internal investigation:
  - Forensic review of trading
  - Communication analysis
  - Timeline construction
  - Witness identification

â–¡ Employee status:
  - Administrative leave (paid)
  - No contact with firm
  - Legal representation advised
  - No statements

â–¡ Regulatory strategy:
  - Self-reporting analysis
  - Cooperation benefits
  - Wells notice preparation
  - Settlement considerations
```

### PLAYBOOK 5: MEDIA INQUIRY

**IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (Within 1 Hour):**

```
MEDIA RESPONSE PROTOCOL

â–¡ DO NOT RESPOND IMMEDIATELY

â–¡ Escalate to:
  - Chief Compliance Officer
  - CEO/President
  - Legal Counsel
  - Public Relations (if available)

â–¡ Gather information:
  - Reporter name and outlet
  - Deadline
  - Topic of inquiry
  - How did they get our name?
  - What documents do they have?

â–¡ Assess situation:
  - Is there an underlying issue?
  - Regulatory inquiry pending?
  - Customer complaint?
  - Industry-wide story?

â–¡ Prepare response:
  - Draft statement (legal review)
  - Designate spokesperson
  - Key message points
  - Redirect if appropriate

â–¡ Response options:
  - Written statement only
  - Phone interview (recorded)
  - In-person interview (rarely)
  - "No comment" (last resort)

â–¡ Post-publication:
  - Monitor article/broadcast
  - Assess accuracy
  - Correction request (if needed)
  - Impact assessment
  - Customer/employee communication
```

### PLAYBOOK 6: CUSTOMER COMPLAINT

**RESPONSE TIMELINE: 24 Hours**

```
COMPLAINT HANDLING

â–¡ Immediate logging (same day):
  - Complaint tracking number
  - Date received
  - Customer name and account
  - Nature of complaint
  - Amount in controversy

â–¡ Notifications (within 4 hours):
  - Branch manager
  - Supervising principal
  - CCO
  - Registered rep (if named)

â–¡ Preliminary review (within 24 hours):
  - Pull account records
  - Pull trade records
  - Pull all communications
  - Identify witnesses

â–¡ Investigation assignment:
  - Assign investigator
  - Set deadline (typically 10 business days)
  - Provide investigation checklist

â–¡ Customer communication:
  - Acknowledge receipt (within 2 business days)
  - Provide investigation timeline
  - Identify point of contact
  - Set expectations

â–¡ Investigation:
  - Interview complainant
  - Interview registered rep
  - Interview witnesses
  - Analyze documents
  - Determine facts

â–¡ Resolution:
  - Findings documented
  - Settlement offer (if appropriate)
  - Customer response
  - Settlement agreement

â–¡ Regulatory compliance:
  - Update Form U4/U5 (if $15,000+)
  - Annual compliance report
  - Retention (4 years)
```
