### SERIES 7 - RETAIL BROKERAGE VIOLATIONS

#### 1. SUITABILITY VIOLATIONS (FINRA Rule 2111)
**Violation:** Recommending unsuitable securities based on customer profile

**Examples:**
- Recommending high-risk options to conservative investor
- Recommending illiquid securities to customer needing liquidity
- Ignoring customer age, income, or investment experience

**Red Flags:**
- Customer complaints about losses
- High concentration in one security type
- Recommendations inconsistent with stated objectives

**Remediation:**
1. Review and document suitability analysis for ALL recommendations
2. Implement enhanced supervision of problematic reps
3. Retrain on suitability requirements
4. Consider restitution to affected customers
5. Update account information to ensure accuracy

**Prevention:**
- Pre-trade suitability review for complex products
- Regular account reviews
- Suitability questionnaire updates annually
- Enhanced supervision for new reps

---

#### 2. CHURNING (FINRA Rule 2111 - Quantitative Suitability)
**Violation:** Excessive trading to generate commissions

**Examples:**
- Turnover ratio >6 annually
- Cost-to-equity ratio >20%
- In-and-out trading patterns
- Switching between similar securities unnecessarily

**Red Flags:**
- High commission-to-account value ratio
- Frequent trades in discretionary accounts
- Pattern of short holding periods
- Customer complaints about excessive trading

**Remediation:**
1. Immediately cease excessive trading
2. Calculate turnover and cost ratios
3. Review all discretionary authority
4. Provide restitution for excess commissions
5. Enhanced supervision of account

**Prevention:**
- Monitor turnover ratios monthly
- Exception reports for high-activity accounts
- Quarterly discretionary account reviews
- Limit discretionary authority for high-risk reps

---

#### 3. UNAUTHORIZED TRADING
**Violation:** Executing trades without customer authorization

**Examples:**
- Trading without speaking to customer
- Exercising discretion without written authorization (FINRA Rule 3260)
- Ignoring customer instructions
- "Recommending strongly" = ordering

**Red Flags:**
- Customer claims of unauthorized trades
- Frequent trade corrections
- Pattern of trades benefiting rep over customer
- Discretionary trading without proper documentation

**Remediation:**
1. Stop all trading in affected accounts
2. Investigate extent of unauthorized activity
3. Reverse unauthorized trades if possible
4. Provide restitution to customer
5. Terminate or suspend violating rep
6. Enhanced supervision or transfer accounts

**Prevention:**
- Require documented authorization for all trades
- Random call-back verification to customers
- Review all discretionary authority quarterly
- Record all trade instructions

---

#### 4. FAILURE TO SUPERVISE (FINRA Rule 3110)
**Violation:** Inadequate supervision of registered representatives

**Examples:**
- No review of email communications
- Failure to approve new accounts timely
- No branch inspections conducted
- Ignoring exception reports

**Red Flags:**
- Multiple rep violations
- Customer complaints not addressed
- Missing supervisory signatures
- Overdue inspections

**Remediation:**
1. Implement immediate enhanced supervision
2. Conduct comprehensive review of supervision system
3. Update Written Supervisory Procedures
4. Hire additional supervisory personnel if needed
5. Retrain all supervisors

**Prevention:**
- Annual WSP review and update
- Regular supervisory training
- Documented review of all required areas
- Appropriate supervisor-to-rep ratio

---

### SERIES 24 - PRINCIPAL VIOLATIONS

#### 5. INADEQUATE WRITTEN SUPERVISORY PROCEDURES
**Violation:** WSP not comprehensive or not followed

**Examples:**
- WSP not updated annually
- WSP doesn't reflect actual practices
- No designated principals for specific activities
- Missing required elements

**Remediation:**
1. Comprehensive WSP audit
2. Update to reflect current operations
3. Designate all required principals
4. Train all personnel on updated WSP
5. Document annual review

**Prevention:**
- Annual WSP review calendar
- Legal/compliance review of updates
- Include all required FINRA elements
- Regular comparison of WSP to actual practices

---

#### 6. FAILURE TO CONDUCT REQUIRED INSPECTIONS
**Violation:** Not inspecting branch offices on required schedule

**Examples:**
- OSJ not inspected annually
- Branch offices not inspected within 3 years
- Inspection reports not documented
- No follow-up on inspection findings

**Remediation:**
1. Conduct all overdue inspections immediately
2. Document findings in detailed reports
3. Create/update inspection schedule
4. Implement remediation for findings
5. Calendar system for future inspections

**Prevention:**
- Annual inspection calendar
- Scheduled reminders for inspections
- Designated inspection personnel
- Standardized inspection checklists

---

### SERIES 65 - INVESTMENT ADVISER VIOLATIONS

#### 7. FAILURE TO DELIVER FORM ADV PART 2A (BROCHURE)
**Violation:** Not providing brochure at or before entering contract

**Examples:**
- Delivering brochure after contract signed
- Not delivering annual updates
- Outdated brochure delivered
- Material omissions in brochure

**Remediation:**
1. Deliver current brochure immediately to all clients
2. Offer to terminate contract without penalty
3. Review all client onboarding procedures
4. Update brochure if outdated
5. Document all deliveries

**Prevention:**
- Automated brochure delivery system
- Client acknowledgment of receipt
- Annual brochure update calendar
- Material change procedures

---

#### 8. CUSTODY VIOLATIONS (Rule 206(4)-2)
**Violation:** Having custody without proper safeguards

**Examples:**
- No qualified custodian
- Missing quarterly statements
- No surprise examination
- Inadequate client notice

**Remediation:**
1. Engage qualified custodian immediately
2. Begin quarterly statement process
3. Arrange surprise examination (if required)
4. Provide proper notices to all clients
5. File Form ADV amendment

**Prevention:**
- Clear custody policies
- Annual custody rule training
- Custodian relationship agreements
- Documented delivery of statements

---

#### 9. FAILURE TO DISCLOSE CONFLICTS OF INTEREST
**Violation:** Not disclosing material conflicts

**Examples:**
- Recommending proprietary products without disclosure
- Receiving referral fees without disclosure
- Compensation from third parties not disclosed
- Acting as principal in transactions without disclosure

**Remediation:**
1. Provide comprehensive disclosure immediately
2. Offer clients opportunity to terminate
3. Review all compensation arrangements
4. Update Form ADV Part 2A
5. Implement conflict disclosure procedures

**Prevention:**
- Quarterly conflict review
- Required disclosure checklist
- Compensation transparency
- Annual compliance training on conflicts

---

### SERIES 79 - INVESTMENT BANKING VIOLATIONS

#### 10. REGULATION M VIOLATIONS
**Violation:** Trading during restricted period

**Examples:**
- Bidding during restricted period
- Failure to identify restricted period correctly
- Inadequate documentation of stabilization
- Failure to notify FINRA of stabilization

**Remediation:**
1. Cease all restricted activity immediately
2. Document all trading during period
3. Report violation to FINRA
4. Implement Reg M compliance procedures
5. Retrain all trading personnel

**Prevention:**
- Automated restricted period alerts
- Pre-deal Reg M compliance review
- Dedicated Reg M compliance officer
- Daily trading surveillance

---

#### 11. FINRA RULE 5110 VIOLATIONS (CORPORATE FINANCING)
**Violation:** Excessive underwriting compensation

**Examples:**
- Total compensation exceeds 8%
- Unreasonable terms in underwriting agreement
- Failure to file with FINRA
- Conflicts of interest not disclosed

**Remediation:**
1. Review all compensation arrangements
2. File required FINRA forms
3. Restructure if excessive
4. Provide disclosure to issuer
5. Implement pre-deal compliance review

**Prevention:**
- Pre-deal Rule 5110 analysis
- Corporate finance compliance checklist
- FINRA filing calendar
- Independent fairness opinions

---

### LIFE & HEALTH INSURANCE VIOLATIONS

#### 12. TWISTING
**Violation:** Misrepresenting policy to induce replacement

**Examples:**
- Exaggerating benefits of new policy
- Minimizing costs of replacement
- Omitting surrender charges
- False comparisons

**Remediation:**
1. Stop replacement immediately
2. Restore original policy if possible
3. Provide accurate comparison
4. Reimburse customer for losses
5. Retrain agent or terminate

**Prevention:**
- Mandatory replacement disclosure forms
- Side-by-side policy comparisons
- Supervisory review of all replacements
- Replacement ratio monitoring

---

#### 13. REBATING
**Violation:** Returning part of premium as inducement

**Examples:**
- Offering cash kickbacks
- Providing services at below market rates
- Splitting commissions with customer
- Valuable gifts exceeding limits

**Remediation:**
1. Cease all rebating immediately
2. Recover any rebates provided
3. Report to state insurance department
4. Implement anti-rebating controls
5. Retrain or terminate agent

**Prevention:**
- Clear anti-rebating policy
- Gift and compensation limits
- Regular audits of compensation
- Anonymous complaint hotline

---

#### 14. FAILURE TO MAINTAIN CONTINUING EDUCATION
**Violation:** License lapse due to CE non-compliance

**Examples:**
- Missing renewal deadline
- Insufficient CE hours
- Wrong type of CE courses
- No ethics course completed

**Remediation:**
1. Complete all required CE immediately
2. Apply for license reinstatement
3. Notify all insurers of lapse
4. Document completion
5. Cease all insurance activity until reinstated

**Prevention:**
- CE tracking system
- 90-day advance renewal reminders
- Approved course list
- Automatic enrollment in required courses
